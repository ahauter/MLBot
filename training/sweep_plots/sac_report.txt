COMPARISON WITH REINFORCE SWEEP (35 experiments):
  REINFORCE spectral (any gamma/lr/hidden):
    Best paddle WR: 19%  Best goal WR: 51%
  REINFORCE raw (gamma=0.99):
    Best paddle WR: 23%  Best goal WR: 49%

SAC ADVANTAGE OVER REINFORCE:
  Spectral paddle: 86% vs 19% (+67 percentage points)
  Spectral goal:  100% vs 51% (+49 percentage points)

WHY SAC WORKS WHERE REINFORCE FAILS:
  Spectral features have lag-1 autocorrelation of 0.02
  (vs raw 0.99). REINFORCE averages gradients over 300+
  step episodes — decorrelated obs causes cancellation.
  SAC uses per-step TD updates with replay buffer —
  each transition is learned from independently.

REWARD WAVEPACKET IMPACT:
  Without reward updates: 44% peak paddle
  With reward updates:    86% peak paddle (+42pp)
  The reward wavepacket builds a continuous reward
  landscape from sparse signals — critical for the
  spectral encoder to provide useful features.

NEXT STEP: Learnable conv encoder
  Current conv filters are random He-initialized
  projections that never update. Making them learnable
  (backprop from SAC critic loss) should improve
  feature quality significantly.
